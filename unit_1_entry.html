Unit 1: Foundations of Scripture Study

Exegesis, Eisegesis, and the Storyline of the Book of Mormon

Introduction

This entry explores what I learned in Unit 1 of REL A 275, focusing on two main themes: the storyline of the Book of Mormon and the tools of exegesis and eisegesis. It’s helped me understand how knowing context and applying truth personally can transform how I read scripture.

Image: Jesus Christ visits the Americas (3 Nephi 11). This is the climax of the Book of Mormon's storyline and a key focus of our study.

Precepts and Doctrines

One major precept I took away is the importance of serious, daily scripture study. Alma 17:2 shows that diligent searching led the sons of Mosiah to strong faith. That tells me it’s not about knowing facts—it’s about being spiritually prepared. Another is the importance of not comparing ourselves to others and instead trusting Christ’s grace. This principle stood out in class and reminded me to focus on progress, not perfection.

Image: Lehi’s family journeying to the promised land — a core part of the Book of Mormon storyline we reviewed.

Exegesis vs. Eisegesis

This unit also introduced two powerful frameworks: exegesis (understanding scripture in its original context) and eisegesis (applying scripture personally). One example I loved was Paul’s use of the word “temple.” In 1 Corinthians 3, he’s talking about the Church community. In 1 Corinthians 6, it’s our personal bodies. Knowing the difference makes both passages richer and more applicable.

"Exegesis gives us a foundation. Eisegesis helps us apply it in real life. We need both to fully understand God’s word."

Why This Matters to Me

What hit me hardest was the idea that faith grows gradually. Like Alma 32 teaches, it starts like a seed. It needs time, care, and consistency. I’ve been impatient before, but now I’m learning to trust that spiritual growth comes over time, just like a plant we nourish. This truth applies to every principle we studied this week: repentance, scripture study, grace, and testimony.

Image: Alma’s seed of faith growing into a tree of life. This metaphor changed how I think about my own testimony.

Personal Application

  • I will use walking or commuting time to pray or ponder like Lehi did.
  • I will stop comparing my spiritual growth to others and trust Jesus Christ’s timeline for me.
  • I will plant the “word” in my heart by slowing down and actually studying the scriptures, not just reading them.

This unit showed me that the Book of Mormon isn’t just ancient history. It’s alive, and it’s meant for me. Now, I’m excited to keep digging in—not just to understand it better, but to change my heart through it.
